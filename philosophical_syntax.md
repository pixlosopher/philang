## Part VII: Generative Principles

### Principle 1: Every Expression is Contestable

Any well-formed expression can be:
- Negated
- Questioned  
- Contrasted with alternatives
- Rewritten in different vocabulary
- Temporally relocated

- Self-reference produces self-constitution problems
- Category violations produce new concepts
- Impossible combinations map conceptual limits

### Principle 3: Multiple Descriptions Illuminate

The same phenomenon admits multiple formalizations:
- Each reveals different aspects
- Translation between them = philosophical work
- No single "correct" formalization

### Principle 4: Syntax Embodies Content

The form of this language reflects its content:
- Multiplicity of primitives = ontological multiplicity
- Combinatorial freedom = productive difference
- Non-resolution = post-metaphysical thinking
